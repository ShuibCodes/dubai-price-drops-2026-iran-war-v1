Hi Andrew,

Great questions:

1. **DLD fee**: Fixed by law at 4%. Non-negotiable. Some developers offer to split it (paying 2% themselves) on off-plan, but for secondary market resale, the buyer pays the full 4%. It's a one-time cost.

2. **Service charge comparison**: Creek Gate at AED 20/sqft is mid-range for Emaar. For comparison:
   - Downtown (Burj Vista, Boulevard Point): AED 22-28/sqft
   - Dubai Hills (Park Point): AED 18-20/sqft
   - Marina (Emaar 6 towers): AED 16-18/sqft
   Creek Gate is competitive for a new-ish waterfront building.

3. **Cash timeline**: From signing the MOU, a cash deal typically completes in 2-3 weeks. Breakdown: NOC from Emaar (3-5 business days), DLD transfer appointment (1-2 business days after NOC), done. I've seen it happen in 10 days when all parties are motivated.

4. **Service charge increases**: RERA regulates service charges and they must be approved annually. Increases are typically 3-5% per year. Significant jumps (10%+) are rare and usually happen when a building has major maintenance work (facade repair, elevator replacement, etc.). Creek Gate is new (completed 2023), so major maintenance is years away.
