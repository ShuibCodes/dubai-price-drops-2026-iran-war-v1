**[00:05:40] Nadia Hasan:**
Thanks Layla. Next question — **Jonathan Birch** asks: "I'm a first-time buyer. I have no idea where to start. What's the actual process for buying in Dubai?"

**[00:05:55] Omar Kazmi:**
Great question Jonathan, and you're not alone — a lot of people find the process confusing because it's different from the UK or the US. Here's the simple version:

1. **Get pre-approved for a mortgage** (if you need one). This tells you your budget.
2. **Find the property** — we handle this for you. Viewings, shortlisting, etc.
3. **Make an offer** — if accepted, both parties sign an MOU (Memorandum of Understanding) and you pay a 10% deposit.
4. **NOC (No Objection Certificate)** — the developer confirms the property is clear.
5. **Transfer at DLD (Dubai Land Department)** — you pay the balance, the title deed transfers to your name.
6. **Fees**: 4% DLD transfer fee, 2% agency fee, plus admin charges (roughly AED 5,000-7,000 total).

The whole process takes 3-6 weeks from offer to keys.

**[00:12:00] Nadia Hasan:**
Let's pivot to a topic a few people have asked about — payment plans versus cash. Omar, can you break this down?

**[00:12:12] Omar Kazmi:**
Sure. For off-plan, developers offer payment plans to make it easier. Typical structures:

- **50/50** — 50% during construction, 50% on handover
- **60/40** — more front-loaded
- **80/20** — heavy during construction, lighter at handover
- **Post-handover** — some developers offer 3-5 years post-handover payment. This is attractive because you can rent the unit out and use rental income to cover installments.

For secondary market (ready properties), you're either paying cash or getting a mortgage. Mortgages in the UAE are available at around 4.5-5.5% interest currently. Up to 80% LTV for residents, 70% for non-residents.

**Cash advantages:** No interest, faster close, stronger negotiating position — sellers prefer cash because there's no bank approval risk. You can usually negotiate 3-5% off asking by offering cash with a quick close.

**Payment plan advantages:** Lower upfront commitment, leverage, ability to profit from capital appreciation before paying the full amount.
